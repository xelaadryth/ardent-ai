# The Foundry Gambit
## Encounter Identity

**Type:** Combat / Chase / Moral Trial  
**Purpose:** Force N’Tal to choose between vengeance and protection, triggering his bond with Pyre and manifesting Abrasion through instinctive action rather than destruction.

---

# Setup

Following leads, the party tracks a shipment of humans moving through a dark, abandoned industrial foundry in the Scarstone Quarter. The building once processed crem-iron for the Revolar Guard, but now in disrepair serves as a covert holding and transfer point for political undesirables, criminals, and now also suspected Possessed who are sometimes sold to the Truthkeepers.

The party arrives during a transfer operation led by **Arbiter Jalis**, a Bloodmark lieutenant present during the betrayal that led to N’Tal’s parents’ deaths. Jalis is about to torture a young boy named Jory for information. "Tell me where I can find them. Tell me!" The boy blubbers incoherently.

**Jalis** immediately recognizes N’Tal.

Rather than standing his ground, **Jalis** smirks and withdraws toward the upper catwalks while his men stall the party.

> “You haven't gotten any taller.”  
> “It's a shame I missed you that day.”  
> “Come on then, boy. Or are you going to be too late...again?”

As combat erupts, **Jalis** signals his thugs. One dumps a bag of bright spheres into a master control, another tosses a lantern onto a pile of oil and kindling in the furnace, and the gears and chains of the old foundry begin roaring to life.

---

# Environment & Tactical Elements

## The Conveyor Crucible

A massive ore conveyor crosses the center of the foundry toward an open smelting furnace. Rusted chains drag ore carts slowly upward over molten slag channels.

During the battle, one of the captives — **Jory**, a terrified teenage boy muttering his innocence — is knocked or shoved onto the active conveyor track.

He begins slowly moving toward the furnace intake.

---

## Upper Catwalks (The Chase Route)

A network of elevated iron walkways suspended 20 feet above the factory floor.

- Narrow footing
    
- Partial cover from steam pipes
    
- Several gaps requiring jumps or climbing
    
- Oily surfaces make rapid movement dangerous
    

**Jalis** retreats through these walkways while taunting N’Tal.

---

## Iron Grates

Cranks attached to chains in the upper level drop iron cages down on the players, potentially trapping them. Other party members need to work the cranks (DC 10 Strength) to free them and lock them open again.

---

## Oil-Slick Floors

Much of the lower foundry floor is coated in leaked lubricant and stormwater from recent highstorm damage.

- Movement requires Balance/Athletics checks during dashing
    
- Normal movement is slow and hazardous
    

This is where Abrasion becomes dramatically useful.

---

## Rusty Ladders, Rusty Supports

To get to the upper level catwalk, the ladders are easily destroyed, and on one unfortunate thug just happens to break off early on in the fight. Supports can be cut to drop portions of catwalk onto the grease below.

---

# Participants

## **Arbiter** Jalis (Bloodmark Lieutenant)

**Goal:** Defeat the party or escape, while having fun taunting N'tal.

**Personality:** Calm, cruel, smug, loves theatrics.

**Leverage:**

- Knows details about the betrayal involving N’Tal’s family and taunts him with vivid descriptions of their slaughter to provoke hesitation or rage-fueled mistakes.
    
- Understands N’Tal’s obsession and intentionally weaponizes it.
    
- Has the high-ground, and has a grappling hook allowing him to pull himself to safety.
    

---

### **Jalis**’s Tactics

- Never fully commits to combat
    
- Uses crossbow shots and environmental sabotage while retreating
    
- Uses a grappling hook to pull him safely across the slick
    
- Cuts ropes, collapses walkways behind him
    
- Attempts to force collateral damage during his escape
    

At one point, while fleeing, **Jalis** deliberately strikes a release mechanism causing Jory on the ore conveyor to start moving towards the furnace.

> “Come on then, boy. Or are you going to be too late...again?”

---

## Bloodmark Thugs (4) & Slick Knives (2)

### Bloodmark Thugs

- Hold the lower floor
    
- Use hooked chains and cudgels
    
- Attempt to pin party members near hazardous terrain
    

### Slick Knives

- Mobile skirmishers
    
- Throw oil flasks
    
- Harass anyone attempting to reach the conveyor or catwalk stairs
    

---

## Jory (Potential Fledgling Radiant)

A frightened teenage boy hearing whispers from an unseen spren. Recruited by the [[UosLnowxcsUZ7qi819lHO|Knights of Dusk]] when his spren was discovered.

- Shackled prisoner, attached to the conveyor belt
    
- Panicking and unable to free himself
    
- Slowly dragged toward the furnace intake during combat
    

If saved, he later becomes:

- a lead toward the Knights of Dusk
    
- proof the “Possessed” are not insane
    

---

# The Choice: Vengeance or Protection

## Round 1–2: The Hunt

**Jalis** retreats through the catwalks while provoking N’Tal personally.

Pyre initially urges pursuit.

> “There!”  
> “He runs!”  
> “Do not let him escape again!”

The party fights through Bloodmarks while **Jalis** gains distance.

---

## Round 3: The Disaster

**Jalis** triggers a destabilized conveyor release while escaping.

Jory is thrown onto the active machinery.

Warning bells sound.

Chains begin pulling him slowly toward the furnace intake.

The path to **Jalis** and the path to Jory diverge.

N’Tal cannot realistically reach both in time.

---

## Round 4: The Test

Pyre falls silent briefly.

Then:

> “…wait.”

A beat.

> “We can make it in time, but only if you speak the words. Life before death. Strength before weakness. Journey before destination.”

N’Tal now faces the core decision:

### Option 1 — Continue the Pursuit

- **Jalis** may be caught or cornered
    
- Jory dies in the furnace
    
- Pyre recoils emotionally
    
- No Ideal is spoken
    

### Option 2 — Save Jory

- **Jalis** escapes
    
- N’Tal abandons immediate vengeance
    
- Abrasion manifests instinctively
    

---

# The Snap: Abrasion Manifestation

If N’Tal chooses rescue:

As he sprints across slick steel and oil-coated ground:

- friction vanishes beneath him
    
- sparks trail from his feet
    
- impossible speed carries him across the foundry
    

Describe movement like:

- skating across stormwater
    
- gliding faster than thought
    
- steel screaming beneath him
    

He reaches Jory moments before the furnace intake.

Stormlight bursts outward.

---

# The First Ideal

As N’Tal secures Jory from the conveyor:

Prompt quietly:

> _Life before death._  
> _Strength before weakness._  
> _Journey before destination._

# Possible Outcomes

- Jory survives
    
- Abrasion manifests
    
- **Jalis** escapes with partial injuries
    
- The party gains:
    
    - information about the [[UosLnowxcsUZ7qi819lHO|Knights of Dusk]]
        
    - a moral dilemma if they should turn Jory in to the [[la-VYCfiPyoH3437byMpk|Truthkeepers]]
        

N’Tal proves:

> he can choose responsibility over hatred.

---

## Vengeance Claimed

- **Jalis** is caught or killed, quickly giving up information on Grand Justicar Malice in Jah Keved as the leader of the plot
    
- Jory dies
    
- Pyre grows distant and disappointed at lack of discipline
    
- The Ghostbloods may quietly approve of N’Tal’s ruthlessness
    

The party gains revenge —  
but loses something harder to recover.

---

## Partial Success

Creative plans may:

- save Jory through teamwork
    
- while slowing **Jalis** enough to wound him
    

But unless extraordinary measures are taken:

> **Jalis** still escapes.

The sacrifice should remain emotionally real.

Saving Jory spreads rumors that:

- the “Possessed” can be rescued
    
- strange powers are awakening in Revolar
    

Killing or abandoning him spreads fear instead.

---

## N’Tal’s Arc

This moment becomes foundational for:

- his Dustbringer bond
    
- his relationship with Pyre
    
- his struggle between destruction and responsibility

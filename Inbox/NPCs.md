# Truthkeepers
## Overview

A secretive, militant organization led by [[eHXnOG1j49L2zlPNEP5Xe|High Witness Yeshan]] . They operate on the fringes of Rosharan society, claiming to be the only ones who truly understand the threat of the "Possessed."

## Goals & Agenda

The Truthkeepers' primary mission is to identify, track, and "neutralize" individuals who show signs of mental instability or hear voices—phenomena they claim are signs of possession by dark forces. In a moment of weakness, [[npc_high_witness_yeshan|High Witness Yeshan]] was convinced by a former advisor named Loid to Connect worldhoppers as a gamble to change the worldline of Roshar, based on a premonition of their ultimate failure to contain Odium. They now seek to secure Revolar to protect supply lines for the militant Sadeas groups in rural Alethkar.

## Structure

The organization is highly hierarchical. "Witnesses" act as field commanders and investigators, while the rank-and-file consists of highly trained soldiers who are fiercely loyal to Yeshan.

- Silent Oath (secret power at the top)
    
- [[npc_high_witness_yeshan|High Witness Yeshan]] - The spiritual leader, secretly the Herald Ishar, whose fractured sanity drives the organization's experiments.
    
- Adjudicators - manage big decisions and high-level plans for the organization
    
- Enforcers - hunt and kill Possessed
    
- Witnesses - investigate rumors of Possessed, low-level logistics
    

## Methods & Influence

They operate with a level of autonomy that suggests they have high-level political cover (from the Princedom of Vamah) and are relatively known to the citylords of Sadeas Princedom.

## Allies & Rivals

- **The Knights Radiant:** (Potential rivals) As the PCs and others begin to bond spren, they technically fall under the Truthkeepers' definition of "Possessed."
    

## Current Posture

Investigate and hunt down the Knights of Dusk to fortify their supply lines from Vamah Princedom.
# Ghostbloods
## Overview

A secretive and highly organized faction operating across the Cosmere, known on Roshar by their distinctive three-diamond (triangle) symbol. They deal in secrets, high-value Investiture, and political manipulation.

## Goals & Agenda

In the Vamah Princedom, they are currently:

- **Infiltrating the Truthkeepers:** Using agents like Chip and Karani to monitor the organization's activities and its leaders, High Witness Yeshan and High Enforcer Aylen.
    
- **Acquiring Fabrials:** They have a high interest in Soulcasters and other ancient artifacts.
    
- **Countering the Bloodmarks:** They are currently in a shadow war with the Bloodmarks, trying to get access to Nurian gems.
    

## Structure

- **Local Cells:** Operates in small, highly autonomous cells.
    
- **Initiation:** Requires significant favors or the delivery of valuable items (like a Soulcaster) for full membership.
    

## Allies & Rivals

- **Chip:** A 7th Dahn Alethi nobleman and Ghostblood spy currently embedded in the Truthkeepers.
    
- **The Truthkeepers:** A primary target for observation and infiltration; potential allies since they help keep down knowledge of the past.
    
- **The Bloodmarks:** Rivals in the criminal and inter-planetary trade underworld.
    

## Current Posture

They are looking to track down information on the Nurian gems on Roshar, getting revenge for the backstab of the Ghostblood agent killed on Mythos.
# Silent Oath
## Overview

A shadow organization existing within the upper echelons of the Truthkeepers. While the Truthkeepers handle the identification and capture of "Possessed," the Silent Oath is called in when a target is deemed too powerful or dangerous to handle.

## Goals & Agenda

To maintain the "order" of Roshar by eliminating fledgling Knights Radiant before they can return. They believe that the return of the Radiants will bring about the Desolation, and thus, killing them is a merciful act of law.

## Structure

They are, in reality, members of the **Skybreaker** order. They operate with cold, legalistic efficiency and answer to a higher authority than the local princedoms.

Led by High Enforcer Aylen (secretly the herald Nale).

## Methods & Influence

When the Truthkeepers report a high-priority target, the Silent Oath descends with the power of Gravitation and Abrasion. They do not negotiate; they execute a sentence they believe is divinely or legally mandated.

## Allies & Rivals

- **The Truthkeepers:** Their primary source of intelligence and "ground troops."
    
- **The Player Characters:** Currently, the PCs work for the organization that feeds the Silent Oath its targets.
    
- **Potential Radiants:** Any individual who begins to bond a spren is a target for the Oath.
    

## Current Posture

They aren't looking too closely at individuals, mainly relying on reports from the Truthkeepers, and eliminating dangerous Possessed.
# Knights of Dusk
## Overview

A rebel organization operating out of Revolar, comprised of ousted soldiers, disgruntled citizens, and led by a core of emerging Knights Radiant. They seek to overthrow the corrupt Regent Paladar and protect the city from the Truthkeepers' "cleansing." They are currently supported by Khria, a lieutenant from the Purelake.

## Goals & Agenda

Their immediate goal is to reclaim the Vamah Princedom for the people and install General Spade as a just leader. More importantly, they aim to "refound the Knights Radiant in this twilight era," seeking out spren and those they bond. They currently operate out of a hidden base in the stone forest of [[location_darkwood|Darkwood]] , concealed behind Stormbristle bushes.

## Structure

Military-inspired and highly disciplined. They operate in small cells to avoid detection by the Truthkeeper Enforcers.

## Methods & Influence

They utilize guerrilla tactics and urban sabotage. Their leadership utilizes the "Possessed" powers (Radiant abilities) of Abrasion and Division to strike at strategic targets.

## Allies & Rivals

- **Highlord Paladar:** Their primary enemy and the target of their rebellion.
    
- **The Truthkeepers:** Seen as the "muscle" for the corrupt Vamah regime.
    
- **The Poor of Revolar:** Their primary source of information and support.
    

## Current Posture

Preparing for a major offensive within Revolar to seize control of the city's key infrastructure.
# Peton
## Appearance

A tall man who stands perfectly straight, embodying the "by-the-book" soldier. He has bright blue eyes and neatly cropped hair and beard. He carries a sidesword and a shield, and has recently been promoted from Enforcer to Adjudicator.

## Personality

Peton was a rigid traditionalist who believed that lighteyes exist to guide the "darkeyed sheeple", but has recently tried to become more open-minded as he follows the rules of the Truthkeepers to judge only based on rank in the organization, rather than eye color, gender, or race. He is a stickler for order, structure, and social norms. While he is often furious at the party for causing trouble, he also believes in democracy when the rules allow for it.

## Background

Because he does everything strictly by the book, Peton often ends up assigned to the "outsiders" of the camp—those no one else wants to lead. He struggles to adapt to the changing social landscape of the Truthkeepers, particularly the mixing of lighteyes and darkeyes in their ranks, but tries to be forward thinking. Originally an Enforcer when the party was assigned to him, but he has since been promoted to Adjudicator.

## Goals & Motivations

- **Maintaining Order:** To ensure that society functions according to established norms and rules.
    
- **Leadership:** To guide his "flock" (even the difficult ones) toward the light of the Truthkeepers' cause.
    

## Connections

- [[la_truthkeepers|Truthkeepers]] **:** His organization and the source of his rigid world-view.
    
- **The Party:** The party reports directly to him.
    

## Secrets

Despite his rigid exterior, his commitment to "the rules" means he will respect a democratic vote even if it goes against his personal wishes.

# High Witness Yeshan
## Appearance

Yeshan carries himself with a regal, absolute authority that makes others instinctively straighten their backs. He typically wears the functional, sharp uniforms of the Truthkeepers, often in deep blues and silvers. His eyes have a piercing quality that feels as though he is looking at your soul rather than your face.

## Personality

He is a man of few words, preferring to let his presence command the room. He presents himself as a visionary protector, someone who understands the "true nature" of the threats facing Roshar. He is patient but brook no dissent, treating his subordinates more like tools in a grand design than individuals.

## Background

To the world, Yeshan is the High Witness of the Truthkeepers, a secretive militant group. In reality, he is the Herald Ishar, the Binder of Gods. His sanity is fractured, leading him to experiment with the fundamental forces of Connection to "fix" or "rebind" the world as he sees fit.

## Goals & Motivations

Yeshan’s previous goal was to utilize Worldhoppers—those not originally of Roshar—as blank slates for his experiments. By binding them to the land, he hopes to create a force capable of hunting down those he deems "Possessed." He was convinced to take this path by an advisor named Loid, who saw it as the only way to avert a premonition of Odium's victory. Yeshan however is easily distracted and has already forgotten about the Truthkeepers, returning to Tukkar to lead wars as religious god king Tezim.

## Connections

- **The Truthkeepers:** His loyal military force.
    
- **The Player Characters:** He has "gifted" them language and Connection, effectively making them indebted to him.
    
- **The Heralds:** Though he operates in secret, his true nature connects him to the ancient history of the Desolations.

## Goals & Motivations

# Karani

Dark brown eyes and a short, stocky Alethi build. Karani has a somewhat "unrefined" appearance, taking manly, heavy steps and often appearing nervous. She wears simple darkeyed clothing, often stained with ink.

# Loid
## Appearance

Angular face, often seen with an amused or taunting expression. He has pale blue eyes and white hair (though some recall it being black). He moves with the effortless grace of a trained performer.

## Personality

A mentor who speaks in riddles and taunts. He is a showman at heart, enjoying the dramatic and the absurd. He seems to find the party's struggles deeply entertaining and "wants to check in on them and laugh at them."

## Background

Loid is a figure of immense mystery who wanders the world (and perhaps others). He acts as a mentor to those he deems interesting, nudging events to happen exactly as he desires. He is often "around," appearing in the most unlikely places.

## Goals & Motivations

To witness the development of the party and guide them (often through mockery) toward their destiny. He values the performance of life above all else.

## Connections

- **The Party:** His current project and source of amusement.
    
- [[npc_high_witness_yeshan|High Witness Yeshan]] **:** He once served as an advisor to Yeshan, influencing the decision to bring Worldhoppers to Roshar.
    

## Secrets

Loid is known by many names across many worlds, including "Wit." He is far older and more powerful than he appears, and his "riddles" are often literal truths about the nature of the Cosmere.

# Stackhouse Slavers

## Setup

As the party returns from tailing Chip through the Scarstone Quarter, they pass the [[location_the_stackhouses|Stackhouses]]. They encounter a [[RPZ-tfzIqzRhfboC6cgfN|Bloodmarks]] collection squad abducting children from a family to settle a "labor-tithe" debt. The party likely steps in when the parents resist and start being beaten to death.

## Environment

* **The Stackhouse Alley:** Narrow and muddy, providing Advantage on accidental **Abrasion** triggers.
* **Overhanging Balconies:** Offer verticality for the Bloodmark crossbowmen.
* **Clutter:** Crates and laundry lines provide cover for [[player_character_kuma|Kuma]] to secretly prepare his devices.

## Enemies/NPCs

* **Bloodmark Lash (1):** Armed with a mace and the "legal" debt scroll.
* **Bloodmark Thugs (3):** Armed with clubs and manacles.
* **The Family:** A father, mother, and two children ages 5-8 from the Stackhouses.

## Tactics

* **The Confrontation:** The Hand pulls rank, assuming fellow Vamah-aligned Truthkeepers won't interfere. This gives Kuma 2-3 rounds to equip his **Soulcaster** and **Painrial**.
* **The Accident:** When [[player_character_n_tal|N'tal]] moves to intercept, he accidentally triggers **Abrasion** on the slick mud, sliding past his target.
* **The Bind:** Kuma can use the **Bindrial** to anchor the thugs in the narrow alleyway, preventing them from escaping with the children.

## Resolution

* **Success:** The children and father Falen and mother are saved. The parents ask if the party are [[UosLnowxcsUZ7qi819lHO|Knights of Dusk]] .
* **Consequence:** If the party doesn't hide their identity, the Bloodmarks will report the "rogue Truthkeepers" to their Truthkeeper contacts (adjudicators).
* **The First Ideal:** Prompt for **"Life before death..."** when the party commits to protecting the family against "legal" authority.

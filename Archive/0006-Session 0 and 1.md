# The Truthkeeper Camp

## Intro

* You still feel a bit groggy from the drugs in your system, which made you “easier to transport”, but you feel like you’ve been out for days, if not weeks, judging from the vague snippets you can remember from your travels

  * Glimpses of endless darkness beyond night, floating on a ship that rode on waves of clacking marbles instead of water, the mist of high mountaintops, the sounds of thunder, heavy wind, and rain, and lastly endless plains of baked clay

* You wake up in a covered wagon, bewildered, and still slightly shocked that what you were told was true. The promise that you could start a new life in another world seemed impossible, yet here you are. What do you do?

* Look outside wagon

Just something about them seems off. But your wagon is on a lightly worn path, and isn’t rolling close enough to get a better look.

* Story Time

  * “Once upon a time,” Loid began, “there was a candle who believed he was the sun.

  * ‘Without me,’ said the candle, ‘the world would be dark and blind.’

And in his little temple, everyone agreed. They prayed, studied, and bowed in his glow. The candle was quite pleased with himself.

* Then one day, someone brought in a mirror.

It didn’t shine by itself, but when placed nearby, the mirror threw the candle’s light across the chamber — and suddenly the walls came alive with carvings and murals the candle had never noticed.

* ‘Stop that!’ cried the candle. ‘You’re stealing my light!’

* ‘I’m only showing where your light can reach,’ said the mirror.

* The candle sputtered, insulted. He burned hotter, determined to prove that *he alone* was the true source of truth. But in his pride, he burned himself down to a stub.

* When his flame went out, the temple was dark again.

* Yet on the walls — faintly, just faintly — the carvings still glowed with the memory of reflected light.”

* Hoid leaned in, whispering as though sharing a secret.

* “Now, my dear friends — between you and me — sometimes those who guard the truth mistake themselves for its owners. Don’t be like the candle. You don’t have to make light to help people see. Sometimes, reflecting it where it’s never fallen before is enough to change the world.”

* The men in the camp are armed with spears, and mostly ignore you as they go about unloading crates from other carts in your caravan. A woman walks through the camp carrying a small stack of papers. Most folks in the camp are dressed similarly in gray

  * Looking closer, some of the fancier dressed ones have a patch on their shoulder most have a sigil of an eyeball circled by a chain, centered on a shield

* The sound of barked commands and the hubbub of people chattering surrounds you

  * Listening closer, you realize you don't recognize their language at all, not even vaguely familiar

* Some armed men usher you from the wagon, pointing at a larger wooden building and nudging you in its direction, shouting words you don't understand

* An older but tough-looking man enters your vision striding towards you and the wagons, and the hubbub immediately ceases. All eyes focus on him. Every one of his steps seems to echo in your ears in the silence. He waves you into the building

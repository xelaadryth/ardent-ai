# Ardent Kaelis
## Appearance

An older, eccentric Ardent with a shaved head and the standard grey robes of his order. He carries a massive brass-bound ledger beneath one arm and several ink-stained satchels overflowing with crem samples, charcoal rubbings, and loose maps. He is an Alethi traveler from the capital city Kholinar.

Kaelis’s fingers are perpetually dusted grey from handling crem residue, and his robes often contain hidden chalk marks and handwritten measurements. His sharp eyes rarely stay fixed on people for long; they drift constantly toward walls, drainage channels, exposed stone layers, and anything weathered by storms.

## Personality

Knowledgeable, blunt, and obsessively curious. Kaelis has little patience for etiquette, lighteyed posturing, or idle conversation, but becomes intensely engaged when discussing storms, geology, history, or ancient construction techniques.

He believes the world itself contains truth:

in stone layers, storm patterns, buried foundations, and the scars left by time.

Kaelis treats everyone—light-eyes, dark-eyes, foreigners, or ardents—with the same academic scrutiny. He often forgets social boundaries entirely when absorbed in thought.

Though absent-minded in conversation, he possesses remarkable observational skills and notices details most others overlook.

## Background

Originally from the outskirts of Kholinar, Kaelis traveled to Revolar to study the unusual crem deposition patterns found throughout the Driftwards and northern Alethkar. The highstorms hit the east side of Roshar first, and the weather was too cold in the south for his age anyway.

His research focuses on how highstorms gradually preserve and bury the past beneath hardened crem layers. By examining exposed strata, erosion shelves, and ancient stonework revealed after storms, Kaelis believes it may be possible to reconstruct forgotten settlements and trace the movements of civilizations following earlier Desolations.

Most ardents consider his work tedious and overly specialized.

Kaelis considers it sacred.

As a member of the Devotary of Sincerity, he believes:

objective truth exists physically within the world itself, if one is patient enough to uncover it.

## Goals & Motivations

Reconstruct Lost History: Kaelis hopes to map ancient roads, buried settlements, and forgotten ruins hidden beneath centuries of crem buildup.

Study Highstorm Patterns: He believes northern Alethkar’s storms behave differently than historical records suggest, and suspects these irregularities may foreshadow significant change.

Preserve Knowledge: Kaelis fears Alethi society values warfare over understanding and worries countless historical truths are being literally buried beneath new construction and storm debris.

Find a Worthy Student: He seeks someone driven by genuine curiosity rather than politics, status, or religious dogma—someone willing to pursue uncomfortable truths wherever they lead.

## Connections

[[siGiDFwEuqwUHi0dm-F9D|Iiko]]: Kaelis immediately recognizes Iiko’s discipline, patience, and curiosity as traits rare among both soldiers and ardents. He views Iiko as a potential successor capable of seeing patterns others dismiss.

The Ardentia of Revolar: Kaelis has access to restricted archives, old city foundation maps, and records concerning historical highstorm damage throughout northern Alethkar.

Local Engineers & Stoneworkers: Though not especially respected socially, Kaelis is quietly valued by architects and builders for his expertise in crem behavior and structural weathering.

## Role in the Campaign

Kaelis serves as Iiko’s primary introduction to the Ardentia and its unusual place within Alethi society. He explains that while ardents are legally considered property, they are uniquely permitted to ignore traditional masculine and feminine divisions, freely pursuing scholarship, science, engineering, medicine, and even martial study.

He also acts as an early source of:

- historical context
    
- obscure maps
    
- ancient records
    
- storm-related anomalies
    
- buried hints connected to the party’s prophecy and the lost city beneath the Shattered Plains.
    

Initially, Kaelis believes he is simply studying geology and history.

Over time, however, his research begins brushing dangerously close to truths far older than he realizes.

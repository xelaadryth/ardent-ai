# Description
As the squad returns from tailing [[Chip]] through the [[Scarstone Quarter]], they pass the [[Stackhouses]]. They encounter a [[Bloodmarks]] collection squad abducting children from a family to settle a "labor-tithe" debt.

# Setup
The party likely steps in when the parents resist and start being beaten to death.

# Environment
- **The Stackhouse Alley:** Narrow and muddy, providing Advantage on accidental Abrasion triggers.
- **Overhanging Balconies:** Offer verticality for the Bloodmark crossbowmen.
- **Clutter:** Crates and laundry lines provide cover for [[Kuma]] to secretly prepare his devices.

# Enemies/NPCs
- **Bloodmark Lash (1):** Armed with a mace and the "legal" debt scroll.
- **Bloodmark Thugs (3):** Armed with clubs and manacles.
- **The Family:** A father named [[Falen]], a mother, and two children ages 5-8 from the Stackhouses.

# Tactics
- **The Confrontation:** The Lash pulls rank, assuming fellow Vamah-aligned [[Truthkeepers]] won't interfere. This gives [[Kuma]] 2-3 rounds to equip his [[Soulcaster]] and Painrial.
- **The Accident:** When [[N'tal]] moves to intercept, he accidentally triggers Abrasion on the slick mud, sliding past his target.
- **The Bind:** [[Kuma]] can use a Bindrial to anchor the thugs in the narrow alleyway, preventing them from escaping with the children.

# Resolution
- **Success:** The children and father [[Falen]] and mother are saved. The parents ask if the party are [[Knights of Dusk]].
- **Consequence:** If the party doesn't hide their identity, the [[Bloodmarks]] will report the "rogue Truthkeepers" to their [[Truthkeepers|Truthkeeper]] contacts like [[Adjudicator Peton]].
- **The First Ideal:** Prompt for "Life before death..." when the party commits to protecting the family against "legal" authority.
